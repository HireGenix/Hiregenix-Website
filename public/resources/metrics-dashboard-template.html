Introduction

Key Metrics Included

Efficiency Metrics

  • Time to Fill: Average number of days from job opening to offer acceptance
  • Time to Hire: Average number of days from application to offer acceptance
  • Cost per Hire: Total recruitment costs divided by number of hires
  • Recruiter Efficiency: Number of hires per recruiter
  • Application Completion Rate: Percentage of started applications that are completed

Sourcing Metrics

  • Source of Hire: Distribution of successful hires by recruitment channel
  • Source Conversion Rate: Percentage of applicants from each source that are hired
  • Source Time to Fill: Average time to fill positions by recruitment channel
  • Source Cost per Hire: Average cost per hire by recruitment channel
  • Candidate Pipeline Growth: Increase in candidate database over time

Quality Metrics

  • Quality of Hire: Composite score based on performance reviews, manager satisfaction, and cultural fit
  • First-Year Retention Rate: Percentage of new hires still with the company after one year
  • Hiring Manager Satisfaction: Rating of satisfaction with the recruitment process and candidates
  • New Hire Performance: Average performance rating of new hires
  • Offer Acceptance Rate: Percentage of job offers that are accepted

Candidate Experience Metrics

  • Candidate Satisfaction Score: Rating from candidate surveys
  • Application Drop-Off Rate: Percentage of candidates who start but don't complete the application
  • Interview to Offer Ratio: Number of interviews conducted per offer made
  • Time in Stage: Average time candidates spend in each recruitment stage
  • Candidate Net Promoter Score (NPS): Likelihood of candidates to recommend your company

Diversity and Inclusion Metrics

  • Diversity of Applicants: Demographic breakdown of applicant pool
  • Diversity of Hires: Demographic breakdown of successful hires
  • Stage Conversion by Demographics: Conversion rates at each stage by demographic group
  • Diversity Sourcing Effectiveness: Effectiveness of diversity-focused sourcing channels
  • Inclusive Hiring Score: Composite score measuring inclusivity of hiring practices

Dashboard Customization Guide

How to Customize This Template

  1. Download the Template: Choose either the Excel or Google Sheets version based on your preference.
  2. Input Your Data: Replace the sample data with your organization's recruitment metrics.
  3. Adjust Metrics: Add or remove metrics based on what's most relevant to your organization.
  4. Customize Visuals: Modify charts, graphs, and tables to highlight your key insights.
  5. Set Targets: Define target values for each metric to track performance against goals.
  6. Automate Data Collection: Where possible, set up integrations with your ATS or HRIS to automate data updates.

Recommended Customizations by Industry

Technology

Focus on technical hiring metrics such as technical assessment scores, time to fill for specialized roles, and diversity in technical positions.

Healthcare

Emphasize credentials verification time, compliance metrics, and retention rates for critical healthcare positions.

Retail

Highlight seasonal hiring efficiency, time to productivity, and store-level hiring performance.

Financial Services

Focus on risk assessment in hiring, regulatory compliance metrics, and quality of hire for client-facing roles.

Data Collection Methods

Efficiency and Process Metrics

These metrics can typically be extracted directly from your Applicant Tracking System (ATS) or HRIS. Set up regular exports or API connections to automate data collection.

Quality Metrics

Collect data through performance reviews, hiring manager surveys, and retention tracking. Implement standardized scoring systems for consistency.

Candidate Experience Metrics

Use post-application and post-interview surveys to gather feedback. Consider implementing a candidate NPS system for standardized measurement.

Diversity Metrics

Collect through voluntary self-identification during the application process. Ensure compliance with privacy regulations and use aggregate data only.

Pro Tip: Create a data dictionary that clearly defines each metric, its calculation method, data source, and update frequency to ensure consistency in reporting.

Interpreting Dashboard Results

Identifying Bottlenecks

Look for stages in the recruitment process with longer-than-average time periods or lower-than-average conversion rates. These indicate potential bottlenecks that need attention.

Evaluating Sourcing Channels

Compare cost per hire, quality of hire, and time to fill across different sourcing channels to determine which channels provide the best ROI.

Assessing Recruitment Team Performance

Analyze metrics by recruiter to identify best practices and opportunities for coaching or process improvement.

Tracking Improvements Over Time

Use trend analysis to measure the impact of process changes or new initiatives on key recruitment metrics.

Best Practices for Dashboard Usage

  • Regular Updates: Update the dashboard at least monthly to ensure timely insights.
  • Executive Summaries: Create simplified versions for executive leadership focusing on strategic metrics.
  • Team Reviews: Schedule regular reviews with the recruitment team to discuss insights and action plans.
  • Benchmark Comparisons: Include industry benchmarks where available to provide context.
  • Action Planning: Document specific actions taken based on dashboard insights and track their impact.
  • Continuous Refinement: Regularly review which metrics provide the most value and refine the dashboard accordingly.
